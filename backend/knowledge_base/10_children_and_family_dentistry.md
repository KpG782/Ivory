# Children and Family Dentistry

Ivory Dental Studio sees the whole family, and children are welcome from their very first tooth. Cavities (tooth decay) are one of the most common chronic diseases of childhood in the United States — and untreated cavities can cause pain, infection, and problems with eating, speaking, and learning. The good news, per CDC guidance, is that childhood cavities are largely preventable with fluoride, sealants, and early dental visits.

## The first dental visit

Public health guidance recommends a first dental visit **by the child's first birthday**, or within six months of the first tooth appearing — whichever comes first. That first visit is short and gentle: a lap exam, a look at how the teeth are coming in, and mostly coaching for parents on brushing, fluoride, bottles, and snacking. Early visits build familiarity, so the dentist's office never becomes a scary place.

## Why baby teeth matter

Baby (primary) teeth are not "just practice teeth." They let children chew and speak properly, and they hold space in the jaw for the adult teeth developing underneath. A baby tooth lost too early to decay can let neighboring teeth drift into the gap, crowding the adult tooth that was supposed to erupt there. Decay in baby teeth also hurts, disrupts sleep and school, and the infection can spread — so cavities in baby teeth are treated, not waited out.

## Fluoride: the daily protection

Fluoride strengthens enamel and helps repair the earliest stage of decay:

- **Toothpaste:** start brushing as soon as the first tooth appears. For children under 3, use a smear about the size of a grain of rice; for ages 3 to 6, a pea-sized amount. Supervise brushing until around age 7 or 8, and teach children to spit rather than swallow.
- **Fluoridated water:** drinking tap water in fluoridated communities gives teeth low-level, all-day protection — one of the most effective and lowest-cost cavity prevention measures available.
- **Fluoride varnish:** a quick paint-on treatment at checkups. CDC guidance notes fluoride varnish can prevent roughly one-third of cavities in baby teeth. It takes under a minute and has no taste worth complaining about — much.

## Dental sealants: protecting the chewing surfaces

Sealants are thin protective coatings applied to the grooves of the back teeth (molars), where most childhood cavities start because the grooves trap food and plaque that toothbrush bristles cannot fully reach. Applying them is quick and painless — clean, dry, paint, harden with a light; no drilling, no numbing.

CDC data show sealants prevent about 80% of cavities in the back teeth for two years after application and continue protecting for years afterward — and school-age children **without** sealants get almost three times more first-molar cavities than children with them. We typically recommend sealants when the permanent molars come in, around ages 6 and 12.

## Healthy habits for the whole family

- Brush twice daily with fluoride toothpaste — adults too; children copy what they see.
- Limit juice, soda, sports drinks, and sticky snacks; serve water between meals.
- Never put a baby to bed with a bottle of milk or juice — it bathes the teeth in sugar all night.
- Clean between teeth daily once any two teeth touch.
- Keep regular checkups every six months, or as your dentist recommends.
