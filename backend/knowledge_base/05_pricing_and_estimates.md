# Pricing and Visit Estimates

Ivory Dental Studio publishes its estimate math so there are no surprises. When you set up a visit through Ivory, the assistant computes a deterministic, educational cost range from the fee schedule below. **Every estimate is educational — it is not a diagnosis and not a final price.** The final price is confirmed by the front desk after the dentist has examined you.

All figures are in US dollars. Every estimate is presented as a low–high range around an adjusted subtotal, reflecting normal variation in what an exam actually finds.

## Cleaning visits (routine exam & cleaning)

- **Base fee: $140** — covers the comprehensive exam and standard cleaning.
- **Recency factor:** the longer since your last visit, the more buildup and exam time to expect. We compute the years since your last dental visit and apply a factor of `1.0 + 0.06 × years`, with the years capped at 10. Someone seen last year gets a 1.06 factor; someone away for 10 or more years gets the maximum 1.60 factor.
- **Insurance:** if you are insured, the estimated patient responsibility is multiplied by **0.45**, reflecting typical preventive-care coverage. Self-pay patients pay the full adjusted amount.
- **Range:** the low end is the adjusted subtotal × 0.85 and the high end is the adjusted subtotal × 1.25, each rounded to the cent.

Example: self-pay, last dental visit 5 years ago → factor 1.30 → subtotal $182.00 → estimated range **$154.70 – $227.50**.

## Emergency visits (urgent exam + X-ray)

- **Base fee: $110** — covers the urgent exam and a diagnostic X-ray.
- **Issue factor:** toothache × 1.35, chipped tooth × 1.2, swelling × 1.5, lost filling × 0.95.
- **Pain factor:** `1.0 + 0.02 × pain level` on the 0–10 scale you report, so pain level 10 adds 20%.
- **Insurance:** insured patients' estimated responsibility is multiplied by **0.5**. Self-pay patients pay the full adjusted amount.
- **Range:** low = adjusted subtotal × 0.85, high = adjusted subtotal × 1.25, rounded to the cent.

Example: toothache at pain level 6, insured → 110 × 1.35 × 1.12 × 0.5 = $83.16 subtotal → estimated range **$70.69 – $103.95**. Treatment the dentist recommends after the exam (a filling, root canal, or extraction) is quoted separately before any work begins.

## Cosmetic treatments

- **Treatment base fees:** whitening **$350**, bonding **$450**, veneers **$1,400**, aligners **$3,600**.
- **Budget-band factor:** basic × 0.9, standard × 1.0, premium × 1.3. The band reflects material and scope choices — for example, take-home whitening versus in-office, or the number of teeth included in bonding or veneer plans.
- **Timeline factor:** asap × 1.1 (priority scheduling), this month × 1.0, flexible × 0.95.
- **Insurance never applies to cosmetic work** — cosmetic treatment is always self-pay.
- **Range:** ±15% around the adjusted subtotal, i.e. low = subtotal × 0.85 and high = subtotal × 1.15, rounded to the cent.

Example: whitening, standard band, flexible timeline → 350 × 1.0 × 0.95 = $332.50 subtotal → estimated range **$282.62 – $382.37**.

## What estimates do and do not include

Included: the visit described above (exam, cleaning or X-ray, or the named cosmetic treatment at consultation-planning level). Not included: additional treatment discovered during the exam, sedation, lab fees for revised cosmetic plans, or specialist referrals — all of which are quoted in writing before you commit.

## Why the math is deterministic

Ivory's estimates are computed by fixed formulas, never improvised by the AI. Asking twice with the same details always produces the same range, and the front desk team sees exactly the same numbers you do.
