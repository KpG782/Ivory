# Routine Checkups and Cleanings

Regular dental visits are one of the most effective ways to protect your oral health. Most dental problems — tooth decay and gum disease in particular — develop slowly and quietly, and are far easier and cheaper to treat when caught early. Public health guidance from the CDC and the National Institute of Dental and Craniofacial Research (NIDCR) recommends seeing a dentist regularly, in addition to daily brushing and flossing at home.

## What happens during a routine visit

A typical exam-and-cleaning appointment at Ivory Dental Studio takes about 60 minutes and includes:

1. **Health history review.** We update your medical history and medication list, since conditions like diabetes and medications that cause dry mouth affect oral health.
2. **Examination.** The dentist checks each tooth for decay, existing fillings for wear, and your gums for redness, swelling, and bleeding — early signs of gum disease. The exam also includes a brief oral cancer screening of your lips, tongue, cheeks, and throat.
3. **X-rays when needed.** Digital X-rays help reveal decay between teeth and bone changes that cannot be seen directly. They are taken based on your individual risk, not automatically at every visit.
4. **Scaling.** A hygienist removes plaque and tartar (hardened plaque, also called calculus) from your teeth, especially along and just below the gumline. Tartar cannot be removed by brushing at home — only professional instruments can take it off.
5. **Polishing and fluoride.** Teeth are polished to remove surface stains, and a fluoride treatment may be applied to strengthen enamel, particularly for patients at higher risk of cavities.

## Why plaque and tartar matter

Plaque is a sticky film of bacteria that constantly forms on teeth. When you eat or drink sugary or starchy things, plaque bacteria produce acids that attack tooth enamel, which over time causes tooth decay. Plaque that stays on teeth also inflames the gums, leading to gingivitis — the earliest, reversible stage of gum disease. When plaque hardens into tartar, it becomes a rough surface that collects even more plaque, which is why professional cleanings matter.

## How often should you come in

Many patients do well with a checkup and cleaning every six months. Your dentist may recommend a different interval based on your history: patients with gum disease, frequent cavities, dry mouth, diabetes, or tobacco use often benefit from more frequent visits, while some low-risk patients can safely go longer between cleanings.

If it has been years since your last visit, that is not a problem — the first appointment simply focuses on a thorough exam and a plan, and the fee estimate accounts for the extra time a longer gap can require.

## Daily care between visits

Consistent home care, in line with CDC oral health guidance, is what keeps cleanings easy:

- Brush twice a day for two minutes with a fluoride toothpaste.
- Clean between your teeth daily with floss or interdental brushes.
- Drink fluoridated tap water where available.
- Limit sugary drinks and frequent snacking — sipping soda or juice all day gives plaque bacteria a constant fuel supply.
- Do not smoke or use tobacco products; tobacco is a major risk factor for gum disease and oral cancer.
