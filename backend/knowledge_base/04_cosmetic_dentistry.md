# Cosmetic Dentistry — Whitening, Veneers, Aligners, and Bonding

Cosmetic dentistry at Ivory Dental Studio covers four treatments: professional teeth whitening, porcelain veneers, clear aligners, and composite bonding. Every cosmetic case starts with a consultation with Dr. Daniel Reyes — an exam, photos, and an honest conversation about goals, options, and costs. Cosmetic work is only performed on a healthy mouth: active decay or gum disease is treated first.

## Professional teeth whitening

Whitening lightens stains in natural tooth enamel. Two options are offered:

- **In-office whitening** — a supervised session using a stronger whitening gel, typically finished in about 90 minutes, with the most noticeable single-visit change.
- **Take-home trays** — custom-fitted trays with professional-strength gel worn at home over one to two weeks, giving gradual, controllable results.

What to know before whitening:

- Whitening works on **natural enamel only**. Crowns, veneers, and fillings do not change color, so visible dental work may need replacement to match afterward.
- Surface (extrinsic) stains from coffee, tea, red wine, and tobacco respond best. Deeper (intrinsic) discoloration — from injury, certain medications, or aging — responds more slowly, and grayish tones lighten less predictably than yellow tones.
- Temporary tooth sensitivity and mild gum irritation are the most common side effects and usually resolve within a few days. Fluoride toothpaste for sensitive teeth helps.
- Results are not permanent; touch-ups every 6–24 months are typical depending on diet and habits.

## Porcelain veneers

Veneers are thin, custom-made porcelain shells bonded to the front of teeth to change their color, shape, or length. They suit deeply stained teeth that resist whitening, chipped or worn edges, and small gaps. The process usually takes two to three visits: preparation (a thin layer of enamel is removed — this step is **irreversible**), temporary veneers while the lab fabricates the final set, then bonding and bite adjustment. With good care, veneers commonly last ten years or more, but they can chip and are not repaired by whitening.

## Clear aligners

Aligners are a series of removable, nearly invisible plastic trays that gradually move teeth. They suit mild to moderate crowding, spacing, and relapse after past orthodontics; complex bite problems may still need conventional orthodontics, and we will say so at the consultation. Expectations:

- Trays are worn 20–22 hours a day, removed only for eating and cleaning.
- Typical treatment runs 6–18 months, with a new tray set every one to two weeks.
- A retainer is required after treatment to hold the result.
- Because trays are removable, success depends heavily on consistent wear.

## Composite bonding

Bonding uses tooth-colored composite resin, sculpted and hardened directly on the tooth, to repair small chips, close minor gaps, and reshape edges. It is the most economical cosmetic option, usually completed in a single visit with little or no enamel removal. Trade-offs: composite can stain and chip over time, and typically needs refreshing every five to ten years.

## Choosing between options

As a rule of thumb: whitening changes **color** only; bonding makes **small shape repairs** affordably; veneers deliver the **largest color-and-shape change** at the highest cost and commitment; aligners change **position**, not color. Many smile plans combine treatments — for example, aligners first, then whitening.
